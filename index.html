Saturday October 3rd, 2015
* registration closes September 15th

What

Learn real technology from real startups.

Startup Slam is a one day series of technical workshops hosted by leaders in the Victoria startup community. Come out and learn what programming for a startup is actually like!

Workshops are free to attend and highly interactive - you'll be actually coding, and mentors from great startups will be around to help you out. Topics range from Advanced AI Prototyping to Bootstrapping Your Own Startup!

It all happens on October 3rd and students and developers of all skill levels are invited to attend. Lunch, snacks, and coffee will be provided to keep you energized throughout the day.

Register early and get a limited edition Startup Slam T-Shirt!

Who

Anyone who can code, wants to learn, or is interested in the world of tech startups. This includes students from the University of Victoria, Camosun College, Vancouver Island University and Royal Roads University.

You don't have to be a student to attend! Everyone is welcome.

Developers and mentors from more than 10 local startups, tech companies, and community groups will be around to help you debug, answer questions, and hand out swag.

Why

Because coding assignments and personal projects are very different from code in the wild. We want to bridge the gap between what it means to learn to program, and what it means to actually create software customers get excited about.

You'll also have the opportunity to meet local startup founders and their early employees, people who've actually built meaningful startups from the ground up.

Workshops

Session One
Wake Me Up, Before You Golang?
Gregory Schier & Matt Harris - Sendwithus

What is Go? Is it more than a reason to create bad puns? Or does it allow you to write incredibly efficient code and "Go" to production faster and easier than ever before?

Learn how the Go language is changing the way Sendwithus creates highly scalable web apps. You may even learn to write some code and build some stuff and things.

Customer Acquisition 101
Dylan Touhey - OneNet Marketing

How to acquire and retain customers with pirate marketing. In this fast-paced workshop we'll show you how to acquire and retain customers using the proven AARRR pirate marketing model. We'll then put our skills to work and create live ad campaigns complete with conversion tracking.

To maximize the value of this workshop, participants should have access to a website and the ability to edit the site's HTML code. Working in groups is encouraged.

Session Two
Zero to Slack in 30 Minutes
Jason Trill, Izaak Schroeder, & James Philips - Metalab

Participants will learn how to use cutting edge technologies that offer a top-notch developer experience to build a real-time frontend web application. Led by three senior engineers from MetaLab, we will be exploring technologies like Babel (ES6/7), React, Redux (Devtools), and Webpack+HMR.

Git: Workflows, Teams, and Integrations
Aurora Walker & Rob O'Dwyer - Pretio Interactive

The popular revision control system git could be your new friend, or if you've already been introduced, your bestest of best buds. We'll perform a full introduction, so that instead of just being nodding acquaintances in the hallway, git will becomes the kind of friend who would bail you out of jail.

Session Three
Techniques for Prototyping Game AI
Chris Hoefgen & Graham Davis - Tiny Mob Games

Walk through Tiny Mob’s process for prototyping games and creating simple but effective AI systems. We will introduce techniques for creating a flexible prototype that can be used to quickly test new ideas.

For the hands-on portion, participants will be building the AI for one of the enemies in a game prototype. Learn how to build a game’s AI in layers, and at the end of the workshop test your AI system against others to see which survives the longest.

Customer Experience as a Secret Weapon
Nicole Smith - Flytographer

Design and build amazing customer experiences that grow your consumer business and keep customers coming back for more. Learn how Flytographer used journey storyboarding, customer experience optimization and other forms of growth hacking to build an internationally recognized brand.

Code Review Corner

Have a bug you can't squash? Working on something cool? Want feedback on your project? Come check out Code Review Corner!

Developers and senior engineers from real companies will be on hand to help you out. Bring your laptop and work one-on-one with the pros.
No code base too big, no bug too small.
